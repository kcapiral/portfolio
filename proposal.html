Lasse Pedersen has a clean and editorial design. The project images are nicely overlayed but when scrolled, still remain grouped and organized. Typography is bold and contrasts well against the images.
She Was Only also has a clean and straightforward gallery of projects. Projects contain one image on the home page and centered, putting all attention to the work.
Adoratorio has a simple navigation bar. The 'Works' page lists out the title and image per project in a unique and interactive way. The black background works with the project images and creates a contrast.
Underline Studio also uses a simple navigation. I like how they have a large carousel of their projects on the landing page, then a tiling of projects right below. It quickly showcases their projects all on the home page.
Works has a very straight forward home page. The 'Works' page shows the work by title and image in also in an interactive way. At the end of a project page, the next project shows which is a nice and user friendly detail.
Rezo Zero is an example of a studio I'd like to work at. Their works in UI/UX highly interest me while being creative and aesthically pleasing. Working with developers is also a good challenge and learning experience.
Social Rise is another example of a studio I'd like to work at. We have similar interests in technology and contemporary aesthetics as well. Their environment reflects a start-up experience while being highly creative and innovative.
